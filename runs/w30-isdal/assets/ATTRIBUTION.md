# Asset attribution — w30-isdal (The Isdal Woman)

No verified, license-clear photograph of the Isdal Woman herself exists — she was never
identified, and no public-domain likeness is documented (same reasoning applied to the
subject of `runs/w27-ourang`, where no photo of the actual ship existed either). Assets below
are real photographs of the actual discovery region and evidence trail instead of AI-generated
stand-ins.

1. **img_00.jpg** — `File:Isdalen.JPG`, Isdalen valley, Bergen, Norway — the actual valley
   where the body was found on 1970-11-29. Author: Reinhardheydt. License: Public domain.
   https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Isdalen.JPG

2. **img_01.jpg** — `File:Bergen Railway Station facade.JPG` — Bergen railway station, where
   police traced luggage left in lockers under her false identities. Author: Snorri Matthiasson
   (Snorri-m). License: CC BY 2.5.
   https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Bergen_Railway_Station_facade.JPG

3. **img_02.jpg** — `File:Mount Ulriken - Bergen, Norway.jpg` — Ulriken, one of the mountains
   ringing Bergen and its surrounding valleys, used as establishing/closing terrain context.
   Author: Scott Rettberg. License: CC BY-SA 2.0.
   https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Mount_Ulriken_-_Bergen,_Norway.jpg
